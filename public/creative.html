Creative Exercise

Brief

We are using tables in some places on our marketing site (https://www.netlify.com/pricing/ , https://www.netlify.com/features/adn/ ) but these are not spectacular when it comes to responsive layout. How can we design these tables to adapt better on smaller viewports, and making them more usable for mobile devices?

Solution

As this is more of a divergent thinking task, I am not going to document my process, but rather the final solutions. To do that, here's the desktop table we are starting with:

  1. The first solution that came to mind was to stack the columns into collections we can later reuse. The problem with this solution, though, is that it's hard to compare between the plans or the rows in general and therefore, it's not really a viable solution, as the non-responsive table does a better job at that.

The image on the left is a bit too heavy on the left side and could be too colorful on the eyes of our users. As a reaction, we have the image on the middle, which is the opposite site.

The best compromise happens when mixing both worlds.

  1. The second solution is much simpler, which is to stack the rows into different tables in the vertical axis. Though that's a better way to display the information, and allows for users to scroll, it may be hard, not only to compare, but to scan the information, specially if that table is long enough.

We add a long scrolling list with multiple cards on the left, though because having different cards could get confusing, we unite the cards into one big scrolling list.

  1. The final and used solution is to have a segmented control on top of the table and, though that doesn't necessarily allow to compare in an easy way, it allows to focus on one of the teams, and therefore, see the full condensed information of a table at a glance. Needs to be said that swiping/dragging on the table, would change the segmented control position.

The first image on the left has the easiest visual treatment that comes to mind with the solution above. However, and because other languages could have longer names, and scrolling horizontally is what we want to get rid of, we have an additional row of content.
